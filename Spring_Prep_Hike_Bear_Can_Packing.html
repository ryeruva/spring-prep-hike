Bear Can
Packing Guide

Pack Monday first, Saturday last — first meal always on top. Three bag sizes, three days, zero confusion.

The Cans

One Scout Can · One Adult Can

6 bear cans total — 4 scout cans (3 people each) and 2 adult cans (4 people each). Saturday snacks and lunch are carried in your backpack all day. Only Saturday dinner and dessert go in the bear can SAT layer — plus all of Sunday and Monday's food.

🟢 Scout Can  ·  3 scouts  ·  ~11 lb loaded

🔵 Adult Can  ·  4 adults  ·  ~15 lb loaded

SAT — open first SUN — middle MON — pack last

What's in Each Layer

Day-by-Day Meal Breakdown

Saturday food is carried in individual backpacks — scouts only need bear cans for overnight storage. Sunday is the biggest layer — 5 meals on the hardest climbing day. Monday is the lightest exit day.

How to Pack

3 Steps · 3 Minutes

1

MON bag — goes in first ↓ (bottom)

Put the Monday gallon zip-lock all the way to the bottom. You won't need it until the last morning.

2

SUN bag — goes in next ↓ (middle)

Stack Sunday on top of Monday. It's the heaviest bag — 5 meals including the big climbing day.

3

SAT bag — goes in last ↑ (top)

Saturday dinner and dessert sit right on top — everything else (snacks, lunch) is already in your backpack for the day's hike.

Bag System

Three Sizes · Nested

Gallon bags hold each day. Quart bags separate each meal. Sandwich bags hold one person's individual share. Vacuum-seal the quart bags to save ~40% space.
