## 50-Miler: High Sierra Trip Details

**Route:** Lake Sabrina → South Lake loop via JMT section
**Map:** https://caltopo.com/m/650U050
**Duration:** 7 days total (1 drive day, 5 hiking days, 1 summit day, 1 hike-out day)
**Mileage:** ~45 miles hiking + 5.5 mile hike-out = ~50.5 miles total (~11 mi/day hiking days)
**Target date:** Late July or August 2026 (before school starts, minimal snow on passes)
**Permits:** Required — Inyo National Forest Wilderness Permit via recreation.gov, reserved 6 months in advance

**Day-by-day:**
| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Day 1 | Drive to Sabrina Campground (~7 hrs). No reservations, $34/night. Shuttle car to South Lake trailhead. |
| Days 2–5 | Hike the loop, ~11 mi/day, reaching base of Mount Agassiz |
| Day 6 | Summit Mount Agassiz — alpine start 4am, headlights for first mile on trail |
| Day 7 | Hike out to car (5.5 miles), drive home |

**Mount Agassiz:**
- Class 2 scramble · 13,900 ft elevation · 2,000 ft vertical gain over 2 miles
- 5 nights at elevation before summit provides acclimatization
- Off mountain before afternoon thunderstorms — alpine start is non-negotiable
- Reference: https://www.mountainproject.com/route/123021448/west-slope-summer

**Crew Structure:**
- **Agassiz Crew** — bear CANS required (no bags option)
- **Emigrant Crew** — bear BAGS allowed (no resupply cache viable)
- Each crew: 6 scouts + adult leaders, youth-led with a Crew Leader
- Crew Leader selected during/after prep hike outings
- Reference: Philmont Crew Leader Orientation model
- Food planning baseline: Philmont Menu spreadsheet

**Adult Leader Requirements (50-Miler):**
- Wilderness First Aid (WFA) + CPR certified
- Capable of hiking 8–12 miles/day with 30–40 lb pack
- Navigation: map/compass + GPS
- LNT certified
- Attend ALL shakedown hikes (Point Reyes, Ohlone, La Honda)
- No alcohol/tobacco/marijuana

## Hike 1 (Point Reyes) — Completed Trip Summary

**Route:** Bear Valley Visitor Center → Wildcat Camp (both nights) → Bear Valley
**Dates:** February 14–16, 2026
**Mileage:** ~16 miles total (Day 1: ~6 mi, Day 2: 0–7 mi optional, Day 3: ~4 mi)
**Campsite:** Wildcat Camp Site 003 — two nights basecamp style
**Difficulty:** Moderate

**What made it basecamp style:**
Scouts carried full packs only on Days 1 and 3. Day 2 was daypack-only with optional day hikes, skill modules, and free exploration — a deliberate recovery/shakedown structure before Ohlone's harder ridge traverse.

**Day 2 activities (scouts chose several):**
- Alamere Falls day hike (~1.4 mi RT) — rare tidefall waterfall, best at low tide
- Coastal bluff exploration — whale watching in February, navigation practice
- Wildcat Beach — beach cleanup service project, journaling, sand structures
- Wilderness skills: map & compass, first aid scenarios, knots, stove safety, bear can packing, LNT stations
- Optional longer hikes north (toward Stewart/Double Point) or south (toward Coast Camp) 4–7 mi RT

**Key campsite features (Wildcat Camp):**
- Water spigot on site
- Bear storage lockers
- Direct access to Wildcat Beach
- Path to Alamere Falls from camp
- Group-friendly layout with ocean views

## Key Decisions That Worked Well

| Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|
| No hot breakfast — grab & go bars | Faster camp breakdown, no fuel for food, scouts eat while packing |
| Same menu all cans, qty differs by can size | Simplifies packing, reduces confusion for 11-year-olds |
| Reverse-stack packing (SAT on top, MON at bottom) | First meal always accessible without digging |
| Nested zip-lock bags (gallon=day, quart=meal, sandwich=person) | 11-year-olds can pack independently with zero guesswork |
| Vacuum seal the medium meal bags | Saves ~40% can volume vs regular zip-lock — critical for bear can space |
| Adults bring own coffee/tea | Eliminates one SKU, adults prefer their own brand |
| No group reserve | Keeps weight down; adults carry protein bars anyway |
| Freeze-dried dinners (Peak Refuel) | Best flavor-to-weight ratio; 1.5 svgs/pouch |
| Allergy scout: personal labeled swap bag | Main plan unchanged for 19 others; scout is self-sufficient |
| MyPlate diagrams in HTML menu | Visual food group analysis — good for BSA nutrition requirements |
| Separate HTML: crew menu + allergy scout menu | Clean, no allergy callouts cluttering the main menu |

## Lessons Learned

| Item | Note |
|---|---|
| Honey Stinger contingency | Always confirm Crème Brûlée label for cashews before trip — Sprouts was out of stock |
| GoMacro > No Nuts! | Better flavor/macros for allergy breakfast swap at similar price |
| Bulk Gatorade | 40-ct packs at Walmart are significantly cheaper per pack than Amazon |
| Peak Refuel member discount | ~10% off on orders — worth joining before ordering dinners |
| Sports Basement Basementeer | ~10% off jerky — worth using for bulk jerky orders |
| StarKist white chicken | Order the 12-pack, not 8 × individual; big unit price difference |
| ProBar variety pack | Order Fan Favorites variety alongside single flavor to give scouts options |
| Tortilla count math | 8 adult / 6 scout per dinner appearance — confirm against total before ordering |
| Dried mango | Buy 2 lb bulk on Amazon, portion into 1 oz bags — much cheaper than individual snack packs |
| Banana chips | 64 oz bulk Amazon bag — far cheaper than pre-portioned |
| SunButter vs Once Again | SunButter 6-ct squeeze pack (Sprouts) is a good allergy sub for Justin's if Once Again unavailable |

## High Sierra Meal Planning Notes (for future Claude project)

**Critical differences from prep hikes:**

| Factor | Prep Hikes (3-day) | 50-Miler High Sierra |
|---|---|---|
| Duration | 3 days | 7 days (6 nights) |
| Elevation | Sea level–4,000 ft | 10,000–13,900 ft |
| Mileage/day | 6–11 mi | ~11 mi + summit day |
| Calorie target | ~2,800 scouts / ~3,500 adults | **~3,500+ scouts / ~4,000+ adults at altitude** |
| Bear container | BV500 (700 ci) per person | BV500 per person (Agassiz Crew) or bags (Emigrant Crew) |
| Food weight | ~3 lb/person/3 days | ~1.75–2 lb/person/day × 6 days = ~10.5–12 lb/person |
| Resupply | None | None — all food in |
| Stoves | 2 per patrol | TBD |
| Summit day | N/A | Alpine start 4am — pre-packed cold food only |

**Altitude nutrition notes:**
- Appetite suppression is real above 10,000 ft — first 1–2 days scouts may not want to eat
- Higher fat content foods (nuts, olive oil, salami, cheese) are more efficient at altitude
- Electrolytes more critical — higher respiration rate increases fluid loss
- Carb-loading before and during summit day (Day 6)
- Summit day food must be no-cook, cold, quick — bars, jerky, trail mix, gels
- Consider adding olive oil packets to dinners (+120 kcal/tbsp, near-zero weight)

**Bear can math for 7-day trip:**
- 1.75 lb/day × 7 days = ~12.25 lb food per person
- BV500 capacity: ~700 ci (~5 lb food typical)
- 12.25 lb food will NOT fit in one BV500 → need 2 cans per person or BV650
- Recommend: BV650 (900 ci) or 2× BV500 per person for the 50-Miler
